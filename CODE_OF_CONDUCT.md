# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Arenamaster is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors, users, and community members.

---

## Expected Behavior

We expect all participants to:

- **Be Respectful** - Treat others with courtesy and respect
- **Be Inclusive** - Welcome all backgrounds and experiences
- **Be Collaborative** - Work together constructively
- **Be Honest** - Communicate truthfully and transparently
- **Be Professional** - Maintain high standards in interactions

### Specific Guidelines

✅ **Do:**
- Communicate with courtesy and respect
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Help others and share knowledge
- Focus on issues, not personalities
- Assume good intent
- Ask for help when needed

❌ **Don't:**
- Use derogatory language about individuals or groups
- Make personal attacks or harassment
- Spam, advertise, or self-promote excessively
- Share others' private information
- Create conflicts or drama
- Violate Blizzard's Terms of Service

---

## Reporting Issues

If you witness or experience:
- Harassment or bullying
- Discrimination
- Trolling or disruptive behavior
- Inappropriate content
- Privacy violations

**Please report it:**

1. **Document the issue:**
   - What happened (be specific)
   - When it occurred
   - Who was involved (if applicable)
   - Any relevant messages/screenshots

2. **Report to maintainers:**
   - Contact project maintainers privately
   - Provide documentation above
   - Include your preferred contact method

3. **Follow-up:**
   - Maintainers will investigate
   - Corrective action will be taken if appropriate
   - All reports are treated confidentially

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this code may result in:

1. **Warning** - Private discussion about behavior
2. **Moderation** - Message removal or comment restriction
3. **Temporary Suspension** - Limited access for period of time
4. **Permanent Ban** - Removal from community

Decisions are made by project maintainers in good faith.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Community discussions
- In-game interactions related to the addon
- All project spaces

This conduct does NOT apply to:
- Private conversations unrelated to the project
- Other individuals' personal conduct outside this project

---

## Specific Situations

### Regarding Game Balance Criticism
- Constructive feedback about game mechanics is welcome
- Blaming Blizzard or the game is acceptable
- Blaming other players personally is not acceptable

### Regarding Other Addons
- Technical criticism of other addons is acceptable
- Saying another addon is "bad" is acceptable
- Attacking addon developers personally is not acceptable

### Regarding Politics/Religion
- Keep discussions focused on the addon
- Avoid unrelated political/religious debates
- Respect others' viewpoints
- Don't push personal beliefs

---

## Special Provisions for PvP Context

We acknowledge that PvP gaming can be competitive and emotionally intense.

**What's acceptable:**
- Calling out opponents' strategies (game-focused)
- Competitive spirit and determination
- Celebrating victories
- Discussing skill differences

**What's not acceptable:**
- Derogatory comments about players personally
- Harassment or threats
- Insults based on identity
- Excessive taunting or bullying

---

## Community Standards

### For Bug Reports
- Be specific and helpful
- Don't blame developers
- Focus on the issue, not fault
- Provide reproducible steps

### For Feature Requests
- Be respectful of design decisions
- Understand "no" is a valid answer
- Explain your use case
- Accept that not all requests will be implemented

### For Code Review
- Give constructive feedback
- Explain your suggestions
- Be respectful of authors' choices
- Help improve code quality
- Don't make personal attacks

---

## Accountability

### For Contributors
- Follow this code in all interactions
- Report violations you witness
- Accept feedback on your behavior
- Commit to improvement if called out

### For Maintainers
- Model the conduct expected
- Enforce fairly and consistently
- Keep reports confidential
- Explain decisions when possible
- Be open to feedback

---

## Consequences of Violations

**Minor Violations** (first offense):
- Private warning
- Explanation of expected behavior
- Opportunity to correct

**Moderate Violations** (repeated or serious):
- Public warning
- Comment removal
- Temporary participation restriction

**Severe Violations**:
- Immediate removal from community
- Potential reporting to GitHub Trust & Safety
- Legal action if applicable

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe you've been treated unfairly:

1. **Request Review** - Contact maintainers in writing
2. **Present Your Case** - Explain your perspective
3. **Await Decision** - Maintainers will reconsider
4. **Final Resolution** - Decision is final
